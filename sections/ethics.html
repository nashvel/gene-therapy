Ethical Considerations

Important Issues to Consider

Question: How can "good" and "bad" uses of gene therapy be distinguished?

This raises concerns about who decides what constitutes legitimate medical treatment versus enhancement or modification.

Question: Who decides which traits are normal and which constitute a disability or disorder?

The definition of "normal" varies across cultures and societies, raising questions about standardization.

Question: Will the high costs of gene therapy make it available only to the wealthy?

This could create genetic inequality, where only affluent individuals can afford life-saving or enhancing treatments.

Question: Could the widespread use of gene therapy make society less accepting of people who are different?

There's concern that gene therapy might reduce diversity and tolerance for natural human variation.

Question: Should people be allowed to use gene therapy to enhance basic human traits such as height, intelligence, or athletic ability?

This blurs the line between therapy (treating disease) and enhancement (improving normal traits).

Major Concern: Germline therapy involves genetic modification of germ cells that will pass changes to the next generation.

  • Changes are permanent and heritable
  • Long-term effects are unknown
  • Future generations cannot consent
  • Could lead to "designer babies"
  • Raises concerns about eugenics
