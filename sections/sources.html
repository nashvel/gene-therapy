Two Sources of Stem Cells

Embryonic Stem Cells

From Embryos

  • Derived from 4-5 day old human embryo
  • In the blastocyst phase of development
  • Usually from IVF clinics
  • Extra embryos created during fertility treatment
  • Several eggs fertilized in test tube
  • Most versatile type of stem cell
In vitro fertilization (IVF) source
Somatic Stem Cells

From Body Tissues

  • Exist throughout the body after embryonic development
  • Found in various tissue types
  • Sources: Brain, Bone marrow, Blood
  • Also in: Blood vessels, Skeletal muscles
  • Present in: Skin, Liver
  • More ethically acceptable
Adult stem cells from mature tissues
